Title: "Wireless UltraSound Powered Diamond based Neural Stimulators.

Acronym:SonoNeuroS

Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowship-

Project: SonoNeuroS (link), This project aims to develop wireless, battery-free, ultrasound-based stimulators using Piezoelectric Micro-Machined Ultrasonic Transducer (p-MUT) technology. By integrating ultrasound powering with p-MUT technology and microelectrode arrays, the system will enable high-resolution measurements of neural regeneration activity. A key focus is on developing wireless neural stimulators to study the effects of electrical stimulation on the functional response of retinal ganglion cells (RGCs). This approach aims to enhance our understanding of how neural circuits respond to stimulation, providing valuable insights for neuroregenerative therapies. The project is led by Dr. Sahil Sharma, a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral European Fellow and Ultrasound Instrumentation and Biosensors Researcher, under the supervision of Dr. Clement Hebert and Dr.Homaira Nawabi's "Central nervous system: from development to regeneration" team. Additionally, a six-month secondment training at ESYCOM, Paris, under the guidance of Dr. Lionel Rousseau, is focused on designing and fabricating p-MUT-based neural implants.

The Wireless Neural Implants " Team is based at the Grenoble Institute of Neurosciences, INSERM U1216, France.
